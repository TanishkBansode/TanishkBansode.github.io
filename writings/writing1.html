Fear

The emotion which drives most people almost all the time, which makes people get out of their bed and do things, which creates highly studious people, and no it's not motivation, it's Fear!

-- Motivation vs Fear

What is Motivation? It is a force that drives individuals to do something in a certain way.

What are things people get motivated about?

  • Reward: e.g., I have to prepare for an interview, so I'll get a good job with good money.
  • Fear: e.g., I have to prepare for an interview, or I'll be jobless and disappoint my parents.

There are other forces too, like habit and "flow," but they didn't exist before and came from the above two forces.

If you are a student, more than half of the A-graders in your class are driven by fear instead of reward.

Seriously, which will make you work harder?

  • Working hard so you'll buy a car, a big house?
  • Working hard so you won't have to stay on the streets and be bullied and hungry?

Let's take me for example: when I study for a test with reward being a source of motivation, half of the time I just imagine myself getting 100/100 and showing it to my family and friends. While when my motivation is fear, imagining myself getting fewer marks and disappointing my parents is uneasy. Therefore, focusing on the task becomes easier.

Let's take the most easily identifiable example of working by fear: politicians.

Politicians 'may' do work the entire year, but just before elections, they go on drugs! Giving speeches, creating schemes, plans, explaining what they'll do after getting elected, criticizing other politicians, making controversies, etc. And after elections? They won, all fear is gone, just throw some celebration speech and go NPC again.

My main point is, most people who you see, or you are one of them, who are A-grade students or people who do stuff they despise/don't like are more likely to do it because of fear rather than motivation.

-- Fear in Our Daily Routine

What's the first thing you do when you wake up? Get out of bed of course, else you'll sleep again. Why do we wake up? Don't we think of sleeping all day at that moment? Then why do we still get out of bed? It's because of fear—fear of losing a job, productivity, studies, time, etc.

All the things we do as a part of routine are/were somewhat motivated by fear:

  • Brushing teeth: Fear of germs, bad smell, or whitening of teeth.
  • Bathing: Fear of being unclean or unhygienic.
  • Sleeping: Fear of not being rested for the next day.
  • Work/study: Fear of being poor or feeling inferior.

-- Fear is Proportional to Money

Big capitalists earn a huge amount of money by using the fear of common people or just people in general. Insurance companies, for example, earn a chunk of money by using fear.

LIC (Life Insurance Corporation of India), owned by the government of India, one of the largest insurance companies, made around 40 thousand crores or 479 million dollars in net profit in 2023-24! The number of people who took insurance from LIC is 290 million, while 9.5 million people die each year, who may not even have an LIC.

People every month give a portion of their money to these insurance companies, where they get nothing 95% of the time. Still, they feel a sense of security when they get insurance. So, people are just paying for mental relief, but are they?

LIC was introduced by GOI in the late 20th century after the independence of India because it was common back then for a family to have only one earning man while having 5-10 children and a wife. And if the main person died, the entire family suffered poverty. Insurance was a big deal back then; it is still a big deal now. But now the insurance companies aren't satisfied with one insurance—they want multiple streams of income. Therefore, health insurance, car insurance, and bike insurance were introduced. They even complicated the insurances by making many versions of it, e.g., Gold, Platinum, Silver. And if you choose the wrong one, or forget to renew it, you get nothing!

Similar techniques are used by product companies. It's known as 'Warranty' or 'Customer Service.' If we buy something expensive like a laptop, TV, or fridge, of course, we want it to work at least a couple of years. Hence, spending a little more money on a warranty sounds like a good option. A thought also emerges in the mind, 'Will I regret not buying a warranty later?'

For smaller items, we are more likely to buy stuff with a warranty than without one, but we still don't use the warranty because we think it's a waste of effort for a small thing. I personally think insurances are just another form of tax with some chance of a lottery.
