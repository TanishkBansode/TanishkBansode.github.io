Are you 'good' at it?

When will I say, if I am good at something? We compare that certain skill with the same standard, which is, is it better or worse than people's average, or your friends. So basically, we compare everything with other people and use the word 'good' instead of 'better' to gain validation that we aren't comparing the skill with others, at least unconsciously.

Therefore, even the term 'good' can't exist solo. Using 'good' is just using 'better' without bringing people into context.

Being 'good' at something is also a very relative term, and also depends on who/where you are saying 'you are good at it.' For example, I can say in my school or family, that I am good with computers or reading, or even writing now (hehe), but I can't say it in front of good programmers, writers, or poetry readers.

Or in school, you were good at Maths, but you're preparing for JEE or SATs, and now you can't say you're good at Maths. In the Maths example, I felt I'm not good at Maths because we got to know that there's more to it, and even got to know the competition and people smarter than me.

Factors of being 'good':

  1. Competition
  2. Society Standard
  3. Audience
  4. Self-views
